NOTE (reported, not registered): the literal triangle inequality over EDGE costs
is violated in 25-29% of triples.  We decline to register it because paying
d(A->C) does not put you AT C -- a cost of zero means A already PERFORMS at C's
level, not that A IS C.  Performance parity is not identity, the edges do not
compose, and the inequality is not well-posed.  The chained test carries the
weights through and is the honest one.
